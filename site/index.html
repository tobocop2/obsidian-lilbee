Obsidian's interface to lilbee ‚Äî a local search engine for your vault.

Ask questions about anything in your vault ‚Äî notes, PDFs, ebooks, code, scans, 150+ file types in all ‚Äî and get answers with the source one click away.

Everything runs on your computer. Your notes stay in your vault ‚Äî nothing leaves unless you ask it to. Cloud models are optional, and only when you choose one.

the difference
without it
  • a chatbot in a browser tab doesn't know what's in your vault
  • Obsidian's search finds the word ‚Äî not the answer
  • when something matters, you open the document and read it yourself
  • your notes and your AI live in separate apps
a chatbot here, a search box there, you in the middle.
with lilbee
  • ask in plain English; get a real answer
  • every answer points to the exact line it came from ‚Äî one click to see it
  • it works offline, on your computer
  • it's right there in Obsidian, next to your notes
one plugin. pick a model, ask.
what it does

ask your vault anything

Type a question in plain English; lilbee reads your notes and files and answers it ‚Äî like having your own private Encarta, built from everything you've collected.

every answer shows its work

Each reply comes with footnotes. Click one and the exact passage opens, right where it came from ‚Äî so you can trust the answer or check it for yourself.

it reads more than notes

PDFs, ebooks, spreadsheets, code, even scanned pages and photos ‚Äî over 150 file types. If it's in your vault, lilbee can search it.

pick your AI, no account needed

Browse a built-in catalog ‚Äî featured picks like Llama 3, Qwen, Gemma, Mistral and DeepSeek, or search the full list ‚Äî and download one with a click. It runs on your computer. Plenty of settings if you like to tinker; good ones already chosen if you don't.

private by default

Your files, the models, the search ‚Äî it all stays on your machine and works offline. Want a cloud model for one job? Plug one in, and the plugin tells you whenever it's in use.

writes a wiki of your knowledge experimental

lilbee can draft linked wiki pages from what you've indexed ‚Äî they land in your vault as ordinary notes and show up in the graph view, alongside your own.

install
slug: tobocop2/obsidian-lilbee
1In Obsidian, install BRAT from Settings ‚Üí Community plugins ‚Üí Browse. It's the standard helper for plugins still in beta.
2Run BRAT: Add a beta plugin and paste the slug above. Then enable lilbee in Community plugins.
3A setup wizard opens ‚Äî pick a chat model and run the first sync. That's it; lilbee brings its own engine, nothing else to install.

lilbee is in active development, with frequent releases; BRAT updates you automatically. latest release on GitHub →

It's early days ‚Äî if it's useful to you, a ★ on GitHub helps other Obsidian users find it, and bug reports are very welcome.
